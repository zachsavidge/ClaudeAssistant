# Standard Business Due Diligence Questions

## Product Suite
- Split of revenue by type (product vs services and 1x vs recurring/re-occurring)
- Split of revenue by product SKUs
- Description of what each product does in a way a 5th grader can understand
- Value prop of the product / each SKU to the org buying
- (If software): Hosting model: where is software hosted / how is it delivered?

## Pricing Model
- Pricing model (what's the PxQ in their contracts)
- Contract structure: duration and billing terms
- Quantum of spend for average customer & large customers
- Qualitatively, do customers view this as a "small cost" or a "big cost"

## Customer Base
- Customer base rough mix - by scale & industry
- Customer base by customer if meaningful concentration
- Qualitative perspective on health of key customers if meaningful concentration
- Customer NPS (are customers frustrated, neutral on or happy with the product / vendor)
- Qualitative assessment on whether any customer is only working with the vendor because of a relationship with the founder / management team

## Competition
- Who are the competitors?
- Pros / cons of solution vis-à-vis competitor solutions
- Win-rate (nice to have; more relevant in growth businesses)
- Qualitative assessment of competitive intensity
- Anything that makes delivering the product / service in question difficult?

## Switching Costs
- What, if anything, is "difficult" about replacing the solution from the perspective of the customer?
- Can we quantify the switching cost? (nice to have)

## End-Market
- Are they gaining / holding / losing share in their market?
- Roughly how quickly is the end market the company is selling into growing?

## Sales Motion
- Sell via the channel or direct?
  - If via channel, is the vendor's contract with the end customer or the reseller?
- For growth businesses (need to haves) — otherwise nice to haves:
  - What's the sales pitch: sold on ROI or as pre-emptive protection (i.e. security)?
  - Is the sale a pull (viral or self-serve model pulls software into org) / push (top down sales motion)?
  - Buyer / decision maker at customer?
  - Source of business: competitive replacement or greenfield?
  - What typically "catalyzes" a new sale?
  - Cadence: does a customer adopt a solution all at once, or slowly roll it out across an organization

## Cost Structure
- Employee cost split: S&M vs R&D vs overhead/corporate function
- Cost structure split: est of growth vs maintenance vs zero ROI spend

## Current Operations
- What data do they currently track?
- What systems do they use?
- What type of incentives do they put in place?
- Anything else that gets at what current processes look like + gives us clarity into quality of operations

## Culture & Employee Function
- Split of employees by function
- Key personnel: how far are they from retirement age?
- Salaries vs market rates: is the company underpaying vs what it would cost to replace that talent? By how much?
- Historic employee attrition rates
- Spend on recruiting / gross new hires over the last 3 years
- Qualitative assessment of corporate culture

## Outsourcing
- Qualitatively, what is being outsourced?
- Quantitatively, what is the spend on outsourcing? How does that compare to the spend on payroll for the relevant departments?
- What companies are they outsourcing to? Are any related parties?

## Financial Stats
- Customer retention annually for last 3 (ideally 5+) years
- Pricing trends for the last 3 (ideally 5+) years
